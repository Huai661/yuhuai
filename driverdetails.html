Driver

Key Responsibilities:

1. Driving and Transportation
Safe Driving: Operates the food truck responsibly, adhering to traffic laws and safety guidelines.
Timely Arrivals: Ensures punctuality by planning efficient routes to avoid delays.
Navigation: Uses GPS or knowledge of the area to locate designated service spots, catering events, or festivals.

2. Setup and Operational Support
Truck Setup: Assists in setting up the truck, including parking, stabilizing, and preparing for service.
Equipment Checks: Verifies that onboard kitchen equipment and storage areas are secure and functional before service.

3. Maintenance and Repairs
Routine Checks: Inspects the truck for any mechanical or operational issues, such as fuel levels, tire pressure, and engine health.
Basic Repairs: Performs minor fixes if necessary or reports major issues to a mechanic.

4. Compliance and Documentation
Permits and Parking: Ensures the truck is parked legally and possesses the required permits for operation in various locations.
Record Keeping: Maintains logs for mileage, fuel, and maintenance schedules.

5. Team Collaboration
Loading Supplies: Helps with stocking ingredients, utensils, and other essentials needed for service.
Customer Engagement: Occasionally interacts with customers, especially during setup or teardown, to enhance their experience.


Impact on the Food Truck:
Operational Reliability: Ensures the food truck is consistently available and operational, reducing downtime.
Enhanced Customer Experience: Timely arrivals and smooth setups contribute to a positive impression of the food truck.
Cost Efficiency: Prevents costly repairs and fines through regular maintenance and compliance.
Brand Representation: A professional driver who engages courteously with customers reinforces the food truck's image.

Salary: RM1300/month
